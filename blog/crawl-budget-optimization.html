Crawl Budget Optimization: A Practical Guide

Crawl budget is the number of URLs a search engine will crawl on your site in a given period. For sites under ~10,000 pages it's rarely the bottleneck — but for large ecommerce catalogs, news archives and marketplaces, wasted crawl budget directly delays indexing of the pages that earn revenue.

How Google Allocates Crawl Budget

Two factors combine:

  • Crawl capacity limit — how much crawling your server can take without degrading. Determined by response times and error rates.
  • Crawl demand — how much Google wants to crawl, driven by popularity, freshness and perceived importance.

Measure Before You Optimize

Pull the Crawl Stats report in Search Console and, ideally, analyze raw server logs. Answer three questions: What percentage of crawls hit non-indexable URLs? What's your average response time to Googlebot? How long does a new URL take from publish to first crawl?

Seven High-Impact Fixes

1. Kill parameter permutations

Faceted navigation (?color=red&sort=price&page=7) is the top budget destroyer. Canonicalize to the base category, block crawl-trap parameters in robots.txt, and link only to canonical facets.

2. Fix redirect chains

Every hop is a wasted request. Audit with a crawler quarterly; redirect A→C directly, never A→B→C.

3. Return real 404/410s

Soft 404s get recrawled indefinitely. A clean 410 tells Google to stop coming back.

4. Speed up TTFB

Halving response time roughly doubles crawl capacity. Edge caching for HTML is the single biggest lever on large sites.

5. Keep sitemaps truthful

Sitemaps should contain only indexable, 200-status, canonical URLs with accurate <lastmod>. A sitemap Google learns to trust gets recrawled more intelligently.

6. Prune the index footprint

Noindex or consolidate tag pages, empty categories, expired listings and internal search results. Fewer, better URLs concentrate demand.

7. Use direct submission for what matters

Don't wait for recrawl cycles to surface your priority pages. Push new and updated URLs through IndexNow and submission APIs so budget is spent on discovery of everything else.

What Doesn't Work

Crawl-delay directives (ignored by Google), resubmitting unchanged sitemaps hourly, and pinging the same URL repeatedly. Crawl budget responds to structural fixes, not louder knocking.
